Deep Learning Part 3 - CNNs


Convolution Neural Networks became too famous these years because of their true capability of detecting features efficiently, we will discuss the working and concepts shortly but before that let's take a look at why CNNs are so famous and important.


Overview & History

Image recognition and object detection is crucial task in machine learning. The history of computer vision starts from 1966 when MIT introduced "Project MAC", which was the first noted attemt for pattern recognition in visual objects.

Then came the era of visual recognition challenges, first of which was PASCAL visual recognition challange, which works with 20 differetncatagories of classes of images. This challenge was conducted each year from 2006 to 2012, and you can see the increase in efficiency each year from the graph below.
After the PASCAL, came ImageNet challenge which became a breakthrough in the field of computer vision because the results achieved and the size of dataset used, ImageNet challege worked upon 14 Million images from 22 thousand classes.
You can see from graph that each year efficiency is increasing and even got lesser than human error and on the verge of perfaction. But the major step taken on 2012 by inroducing AlexNet which was the first attempt to implement Concolution Neural Networks.

  • 2012 - AlexNet - decrement in error from 25.4 to 16.4 which was the maximum noted difference till today
  • 2013 - Zeller and Fergus - error = 11.7%
  • 2014 - VGG Net - 7.3%
  • 2015 - GoogleNet - 6.7%
  • 2016 - Microsoft ResNet - 3.75%
  • 2017 - This year is the last year for these challenge - Good Luck

From 2012 all the networks are using Convolution directly or indirectly which suggests that till today it was the major breakthrough and may be almost optimal option for future applications of computer vision.
